The problem

We are standing at a moment where cultural institutions hold more content than ever,
yet people experience it increasingly alone.

Museums have become spaces of observation.
Visitors move through them silently, individually, often mediated by screens.
What is missing is not information—but connection.

What if the museum was not a place
to look at art — but a place where
art happens between people?

How it works
1
Exhibition
as context

Any exhibition in any museum becomes the context. Its themes, artworks and narratives serve as the starting point.

2
AI navigation +
interaction script

Visitors put on headphones. The AI guide navigates them through the exhibition and invites them to take part in simple, meaningful interactions.

3
People interact
with each other

Participants engage in micro-interactions, collaborations and shared moments — both with others and in response to the artworks.

4
Behavioral data
is generated

The system captures anonymized data about interactions, movement, proximity, responses and collective dynamics in real time.

5
Artist translates
data into visual language

A collaborating artist interprets the collective patterns and translates them into a unique visual language and aesthetic approach.

6
New artwork
emerges

A dynamic artwork — a living mural — emerges in real time, reflecting the human relationships within this exhibition, on this day.

A dual museum experience

Living Field introduces an optional relational layer within existing exhibitions. The museum remains the same. The experience becomes deeper.

Passive visit
The exhibition
as it always was

Visitors can experience the exhibition as usual — moving through the space at their own pace, engaging with artworks, following their own path. The traditional museum experience remains fully intact.

Active participation
A parallel experience
shaped by interaction

Those who enter the system step into a parallel experience — one that unfolds through interaction, presence, and choice. Guided by spatial audio and AI-mediated prompts, participants become co-creators of something that did not exist before they arrived.

Participation is optional. But presence is always meaningful.

Living mural — evolving in real time
The living artwork

A new artwork emerges
from the people within it

Each exhibition invites a different artist to engage with this field — not to illustrate it, but to interpret it. Using their own visual language, the artist transforms collective interaction into a real-time, evolving mural.

It is generated continuously. Shaped by presence. Changed by behavior. No two iterations are the same.

In parallel, each participant receives a personal reflection — a short, AI-generated narrative that mirrors how they moved through the system, how they related, how they chose.

Dynamic & unique every time
Changes with every visitor, every day
Each exhibition creates its own visual signature
Personal narrative reflection for each participant

Alignment with LACMA Art+Tech Lab
Artistic merit

The project is artist-led and rooted in a clear artistic inquiry: how human relationships can become both the medium and the material of an artwork. Each iteration co-creates a new visual expression through collaboration between participants, system dynamics, and artists. Authorship becomes relational and distributed.

Emerging technology

Living Field integrates spatial audio, real-time data processing, and generative AI not as tools for representation, but as mediators of human interaction. AI functions as an active agent: facilitating encounters, shaping participant pathways, generating narrative responses. An alternative paradigm for human–AI interaction that is relational, spatial, embodied.

Models & methods for others

Living Field proposes a transferable artistic and technological framework applicable across museums, exhibitions, and public cultural contexts. The behavioral interaction model, real-time visualization system, and generative narrative layer can be adapted, shared, and built upon. The project also introduces a modular artistic layer for ongoing collaboration and open calls.

Public engagement

The development process is inherently public-facing. Early-stage prototypes can be presented as lightweight setups within museum spaces, functioning as both public engagement and live research environments. Rather than being developed in isolation, Living Field evolves through continuous interaction with its audience.
